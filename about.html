Lead Researcher — Mathematics and Physics

Ms. Laura Sad

Academic Background

High School Diploma in Mathematics and Physics, Escuela Normal Superior N° 6 "Vicente López y Planes" — Buenos Aires, Argentina.

Role in the Project

Responsible for the development and validation of the fundamental physical-mathematical knowledge that supports the Domus Horizon Cognitive framework. Her contribution covers the asymptotic analysis of the c(N) function, the identification of the link with the Kissing Number K₂ = 6, and the formalization of the three phases of the Domus Horizon algorithm's behavior from geometric topology.

Areas of Expertise

Differential geometry · Asymptotic analysis · Geometric topology · Mathematical physics · Computational complexity theory.

Systems Architect — Engineering and Algorithmics

Mr. Gaston Rovetta

Academic Background

Electronics Technician, Escuela Técnica N° 28 "República Francesa" — Buenos Aires, Argentina.

Role in the Project

Responsible for the design of the computational architecture, the implementation of large-scale optimization algorithms (Domus Horizon, DGO), and the execution of mega-scale experiments (N = 50,000 nodes, 8 million iterations). He designed the Quark-Quaternary hybrid architecture and the checkpointing system for long-running executions.

Areas of Expertise

Systems engineering · Optimization algorithmics · Distributed computing · Electronics and control systems · Applied technical education.

Institutional Framework

Domus Horizon is an independent research initiative based in Canelones, Uruguay. It operates without external funding or formal institutional affiliation, under the principle of open and reproducible science.

Personal Note: Both researchers consolidate this work with honesty, consciousness and a genuine desire that the foundation of humanity's growth is knowledge and self-knowledge, making this project a scientific and personal milestone.
