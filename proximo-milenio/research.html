Research

Next Millennium work lines
Dynamic Complexity Optimization

Study of the asymptotic behavior of stochastic algorithms applied to the Traveling Salesman Problem (TSP). The convergence function c(N) that governs the minimum number of iterations to reach statistically stable solutions in large-scale instances is characterized.

Phase Transitions in Graphs

Analysis of how the energy landscape of an NP-hard graph changes phase as N increases. Three regimes are identified: local dominance, cluster saturation and macro self-organization, linked to geometric properties of the search space.

Deterministic Geometric Oracle (DGO)

Development of a deterministic algorithm based on Delaunay triangulation and restricted 2-opt search. The DGO eliminates a large part of the search space without sacrificing solution quality, establishing a bridge between geometric methods and combinatorial optimization.

Mega-Scale and Distributed Architecture

Execution of experiments with tens of thousands of nodes on distributed Debian Linux nodes. Convergence times, acceptance rates and symmetries between independent execution blocks are measured.
